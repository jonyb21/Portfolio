# Lock Gallery Redesign

## Outcome

Replace the current hinged lock renders with one mechanically credible bicycle U-lock shown consistently across a hero image, four cropped detail views, and four in-situ views. Every new asset is WebP.

## Product

The lock is a conventional double-deadbolt U-lock. A removable, blue elastomer-sleeved hardened-steel shackle seats into two recessed receivers in a compact bead-blasted aluminium crossbar. Internal deadbolts secure both shackle legs. A recessed stainless-steel key barrel sits in the right end-cap, surrounded by a thin cool-white LED ring that locates the keyway in low light. Rubber receiver collars and a removable rubber bumper prevent frame damage. The lock has no hinge, cable, exposed fastener, or impossible joint.

The material language matches the portfolio: muted navy-blue elastomer, warm silver aluminium, dark charcoal polymer, and small stainless-steel details. Product proportions, receiver spacing, keyway position, surface finishes, and wear marks remain identical in every view.

## Image Rules

- Photorealistic industrial-design photography with natural scale, gravity, contact, and hand placement.
- One bicycle, one lock, one fixed anchor, and one human where specified.
- The U-shackle must pass through the rear wheel and rear frame triangle and around the fixed rail in locked views.
- The crossbar is separate while positioning and attached only after both shackle legs align with its receivers.
- No text, logos, watermarks, keys floating in space, merged geometry, extra lock parts, or changing product proportions.
- Landscape 4:3 framing compatible with the existing cards and detail gallery.
